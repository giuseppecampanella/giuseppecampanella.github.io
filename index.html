Hackaton

Like@Home

36 hrs no stop of development • March 2019 • Turin, Italy

`Invent and develop new skills for a Home Assistant to assist non-resident students in their home life`

Our idea for this Hackaton was to develop an algorithm that would allow the Google Home to understand who has come home to give them a brief recap of the reminders left by the roommates and more. Through the use of the official paid API we were able to convert the human voice into text and vice versa using a database to save the most important information to be used later (MAC, memos, other). Furthermore, through the use of regular expressions (RegEx), we have managed to have exhaustive dialogues with the Google Home to make it as human as possible. We used C, Python as languages, SQLite database and a Raspberry Pi Zero WH as hardware.
